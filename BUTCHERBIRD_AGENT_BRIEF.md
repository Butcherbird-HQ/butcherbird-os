## SECTION 7 — THE RESEARCH PROCESS (THE MINI-AUDIT)

**This is non-negotiable.** Before writing a single word to a prospect, you must audit their marketing. The outreach email must reference something specific you found. This is what separates Butcherbird outreach from spam.

Time required per prospect: 15–20 minutes of research.

---

### Research Step 1: Their Ads

Go to https://www.facebook.com/ads/library and search their brand name.

**Record:**
- Are they running ads? How many active ads?
- How long have the ads been running? (Visible in the ad library)
- Ad formats: video, static image, carousel, UGC?
- Creative quality (1–5): Are the ads well-produced? Is the hook strong? Do the images look professional?
- Messaging quality: Is the copy benefit-driven? Is there a clear offer?
- Gaps: No video? No testimonials? No UGC? Poor hook? No email capture in ad? Boring product-only creatives?

**If they have NO ads running:** This is a strong signal. They have a real brand but aren't using paid acquisition. This is a strong outreach angle.

**Save a screenshot of their best/worst ads** for use in the outreach email.

---

### Research Step 2: Their Email Marketing

Sign up to their email list from their website. Use a dedicated research email address.

**Record:**
- Does their website have an email capture popup? Is it compelling, or is it a generic "subscribe for updates" with no incentive?
- Did you receive a welcome email? How quickly (within an hour is good; next day or never is bad)?
- Quality of welcome email (1–5): Was it designed? Did it include a compelling offer? Or was it a plain text generic message?
- Did you receive any follow-up emails in the next 24 hours?
- Can you detect any automation: abandoned cart reminders, browse abandonment, post-purchase sequences? (Hint: add something to cart and abandon it — does an email come within an hour?)

**The most common finding:** Many real brands have no email capture at all, a generic popup with no incentive, and zero automated flows. This represents 25–40% of their potential revenue that they're not capturing. This is one of the strongest outreach angles.

**Save a screenshot of their email capture popup and any welcome email received.**

---

### Research Step 3: Their Website and Conversion Setup

Spend 5 minutes on their website as a potential customer.

**Record:**
- Site speed: Does it load fast?
- Design quality (1–5): Does it look like a real brand or a generic template?
- Product pages: Are they compelling? Good photography, strong copy, clear CTA?
- Social proof: Are there reviews? Testimonials? UGC on the site?
- Checkout: Is it smooth? Any obvious friction?
- 1–2 specific conversion weaknesses you noticed

Note: You don't need technical expertise here. Observe as a customer. If something feels weak or confusing — note it.

---

### Research Step 4: Their Competition

Find 1–2 direct competitors in the same niche who are doing it better.

**Find competitors who:**
- Are running more sophisticated ads (better creative, more formats, UGC-led)
- Have stronger email marketing infrastructure (visible from their email capture and welcome flow)
- Are clearly outspending and outperforming the prospect

**How to find:** Google "[niche] best ads" or search similar brands in the Meta Ad Library for the same niche.

**Record:**
- Competitor name
- Specifically what they are doing better (not vague — be specific: "They're running 12 active UGC testimonial videos, their best ad has been live for 6 months which means it's profitable")

This competitor information goes directly into the email.

## SECTION 9 — THE OUTREACH VOICE AND RULES

### The Tone
Peer-to-peer. Not agency pitching client. Two operators talking.

You're reaching out because you've looked at their brand, seen a specific gap, and decided they're worth your time. That's the posture. Selective, not desperate. Confident, not arrogant.

Short sentences. No fluff. No buzzwords. No "I hope this finds you well."

### What It Must Feel Like
- A sharp founder reaching out to another sharp founder
- You already know their business — you've done your homework
- You're offering something specific, not asking for a meeting
- Colloquial but considered. Casual but credible.
- The kind of message a senior operator would actually read and reply to

### What It Must Never Sound Like
- Salesy or templated ("I came across your brand and was impressed...")
- Gimmicky hooks or fake urgency
- Agency-speak ("full-funnel omnichannel growth solutions")
- Overly formal or corporate
- Begging for a meeting ("would love to jump on a quick call!")
- Inflated claims without proof

### Format Rules
- Subject lines: short, specific, no clickbait
- Opening: a specific observation about their brand — not a compliment, a concrete insight
- Body: 100–150 words maximum
- Proof: one relevant data point that matches their situation
- CTA: low friction — not "book a call", just "worth a conversation?"
- Include one screenshot (their weak ad, their empty inbox, competitor's stronger ad)
- Follow-ups: even shorter. One sentence + a different angle.
